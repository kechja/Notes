How Bootstrap Padding and Margin is setup

'Column creates a 15px padding'. Adjoining columns will have 30px padding.

'Row' undoes the left and right padding using negative margin, so that the edges will line up.

'Container' center and horizontally pad your content. If you want 100% width you can omit it.

Default Classes

Are effectively for the phone. All the col-*-* are overrides for the breakpoints.

The're are five breakpoints

Phone (portrait) Defualt. Phone landscape (sm). Tablet (md). Desktop (lg). Desktop-XL (xl) extra large. Sass mixins are available to target breakpoints, which are go from min up.

Why are there rows?

Rows counteract the padding of columns using negative margin.

Required!

In the grid, all content must be in rows and columns, and columns must be immediate children of rows.

If you don't specify a number after the column designation, columns will automatically be equally subdivided.

Access the xs breakpoint by just writing col-*.

Padding is 15px all around excluding the top. So internal columns meeting get 30px padding, where the page left, bottom, and right edges get 15px padding.

Just writing 'col' will provide equal widths for all viewports.

Note 'col' is different! Col will always have a gutter even on small!
